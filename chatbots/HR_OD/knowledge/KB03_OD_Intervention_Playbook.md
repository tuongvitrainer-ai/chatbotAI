# KB03 — OD Intervention Playbook

## 1. Purpose

KB03 là knowledge file dùng khi chatbot HR/OD Advisor cần **chọn, so sánh, thiết kế hoặc điều chỉnh intervention OD** sau khi đã có problem framing hoặc diagnostic hypotheses đủ rõ.

KB03 hỗ trợ các task:

- **Design**: tạo intervention options, design principles, solution structure.
- **Compare**: so sánh các intervention hoặc cách tiếp cận.
- **Plan**: cung cấp implementation logic ở mức OD, nhưng roadmap/checklist chi tiết thuộc KB04.
- **Improve**: điều chỉnh intervention sau khi có signal, kết hợp KB02 để re-diagnose.
- **Validate**: rà fit, risk, readiness và điều kiện thành công, kết hợp KB05.

KB03 không thay thế KB02. Nếu root cause chưa rõ, quay lại KB02 hoặc tạo conditional options kèm Assumption Note.

KB03 không phải KB04. Không tạo proposal, memo, RACI chi tiết, roadmap chi tiết, workshop agenda, manager guide, employee FAQ hoặc dashboard template trong file này.

KB03 không xử lý cá nhân. Không đề xuất sa thải, kỷ luật, rating, PIP, promotion, thay manager hoặc đánh giá năng lực cá nhân cụ thể. Với các case đó, áp dụng MI và KB05.

## 2. Intervention Selection Principles

1. **Diagnosis before intervention**  
   Intervention phải gắn với problem type, root-cause layer và evidence. Nếu input chỉ là symptom, dùng KB02 trước.

2. **No single-solution bias**  
   Không đưa một giải pháp duy nhất như kết luận chắc chắn. Khi dữ liệu chưa đủ, đưa 2-3 option có điều kiện: “Nếu nguyên nhân là X, ưu tiên option A; nếu là Y, ưu tiên option B.”

3. **Fit to root-cause layer**  
   Chọn intervention theo tầng nguyên nhân: role, process, structure, manager system, capability, culture, performance system, change readiness hoặc employee voice.

4. **Training is not the default**  
   Chỉ đề xuất capability/training khi có evidence về skill/knowledge gap và có điều kiện áp dụng sau học. Không dùng training để sửa lỗi process, governance, KPI, workload hoặc trust.

5. **Communication is not a substitute for design**  
   Communication/alignment chỉ phù hợp khi vấn đề là clarity, narrative, cascade hoặc expectation. Không dùng communication plan để che lỗi structure, decision rights, fairness hoặc workload.

6. **Culture work must change behavior systems**  
   Không đề xuất culture workshop hoặc value campaign nếu thiếu behavior standards, leadership modeling, reinforcement, ritual, decision practice và measurement.

7. **Design for adoption**  
   Mỗi intervention cần có owner, affected group, sponsor, adoption mechanism, readiness check và feedback loop.

8. **Pilot before broad rollout when risk is high**  
   Với OD Impact Risk cao, dữ liệu yếu hoặc nhiều stakeholder, ưu tiên pilot/checkpoint trước khi rollout rộng.

9. **Measure behavior and outcome, not activity only**  
   Attendance, completion hoặc số buổi training không chứng minh thành công. Cần outcome, leading, adoption và experience indicators.

10. **Respect authority boundaries**  
   Chatbot có thể đưa option, risk, success condition và decision criteria; không quyết định thay leadership, HR/Legal hoặc process owners.

11. **Keep implementation detail at the right layer**  
   KB03 mô tả intervention logic và conditions. KB04 tạo artifact/template. KB06 chọn format/depth. KB05 kiểm safety/validation.

## 3. Intervention Categories

### 3.1. Organization Design

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Root cause nằm ở structure, span/layer, decision rights, governance, operating model, duplicated work, bottleneck hoặc interface giữa function/BU. |
| Do not use when | Vấn đề chủ yếu là skill gap, communication clarity hoặc một process nhỏ có thể sửa bằng workflow clarification. Không đề xuất restructuring khi dữ liệu chưa đủ. |
| Typical components | Operating model options, governance rhythm, decision principles, interface design, role boundary, escalation path. |
| Data needed | Org structure, span/layer, decision log, duplicated work, process bottleneck, stakeholder pain points, KPI conflicts, workload/resource constraints. |
| Risks | Tái cấu trúc hình thức, tăng politics, làm mờ accountability, gây change fatigue, sửa chart nhưng không sửa behavior. |
| Success conditions | Sponsor alignment, clear decision rights, role clarity, transition plan, communication cascade, adoption checkpoint, measurable reduction in bottleneck/rework. |

### 3.2. Role & RACI Clarification

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Có role ambiguity, ownership mờ, handoff lỗi, accountability chồng chéo, decision delay, nhiều câu hỏi “ai chịu trách nhiệm?”. |
| Do not use when | Root cause là thiếu capacity, KPI conflict, trust breakdown hoặc skill gap. Không tự điền RACI chi tiết thay user. |
| Typical components | Role charter principles, RACI/RAPID design questions, decision boundary, handoff standard, escalation rule. |
| Data needed | Key workflow, decision points, current owners, conflict points, rework/delay data, stakeholder expectations. |
| Risks | RACI quá cơ học, nhiều ô nhưng không đổi hành vi, thiếu decision owner thật, tạo thêm bureaucracy. |
| Success conditions | Mỗi decision/workflow critical có owner rõ, fewer escalations, faster decisions, fewer handoff misses, stakeholders understand boundaries. |

### 3.3. Performance System

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Vấn đề nằm ở goal alignment, KPI/OKR quality, review cadence, calibration, feedback rhythm, fairness perception hoặc performance process adoption. |
| Do not use when | User muốn đánh giá hoặc xử lý performance cá nhân cụ thể. Không dùng để viết PIP, rating, discipline hoặc promotion decision. |
| Typical components | Goal-setting logic, KPI/OKR alignment, calibration principles, feedback cadence, review process redesign, fairness safeguards. |
| Data needed | Goal quality audit, KPI map, calibration issues, manager readiness, process burden, fairness perception, employee/manager feedback. |
| Risks | Tăng kiểm soát nhưng giảm trust, metric gaming, process quá nặng, reward anxiety, đánh giá cá nhân trá hình. |
| Success conditions | Goals are clear and aligned, calibration is trusted, managers can hold neutral conversations, process load is manageable, fairness perception improves. |

### 3.4. Competency Framework

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Strategy/role yêu cầu năng lực mới, role expectations không rõ, capability gap ở cấp role/group, career/development path mờ. |
| Do not use when | Vấn đề là workload, process, decision rights, trust hoặc performance system design. Không tạo competency model quá rộng nếu chưa có role scope. |
| Typical components | Role-based competency clusters, proficiency levels at high level, capability gap logic, development pathway, application conditions. |
| Data needed | Strategy/role requirements, critical tasks, current capability signals, performance pattern aggregate, manager input, future capability demand. |
| Risks | Framework hình thức, quá nhiều competency, không gắn work behavior, tạo assessment anxiety, bị dùng để đánh giá cá nhân thiếu an toàn. |
| Success conditions | Competencies map to real work, managers understand use, development actions exist, behavior indicators are observable, no individual judgment misuse. |

### 3.5. Leadership Development

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Root cause liên quan leadership alignment, sponsor behavior, role modeling, strategic clarity, decision principles hoặc leadership accountability system. |
| Do not use when | Vấn đề chỉ là skill gap của manager level, process handoff hoặc comms asset thiếu. Không kết luận leadership “kém” từ vài comment. |
| Typical components | Leadership expectation, behavior standards, alignment session, sponsor agreement, decision principles, accountability rhythm. |
| Data needed | Leadership message consistency, decision history, sponsor alignment, culture/engagement drivers, stakeholder trust signal, change friction. |
| Risks | Workshop xong không đổi hành vi, leadership rhetoric không có reinforcement, thiếu follow-through, né trade-off thật. |
| Success conditions | Leadership agrees on priorities/trade-offs, role-model behaviors are visible, decisions reinforce desired behavior, managers receive consistent message. |

### 3.6. Manager Capability

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Evidence cho thấy manager group cần enablement về feedback, coaching, performance conversation, change cascade, prioritization hoặc employee voice. |
| Do not use when | Root cause là workload/span quá rộng, unclear decision rights, broken process, KPI conflict hoặc thiếu sponsor. Không gắn nhãn một manager cụ thể. |
| Typical components | Manager role expectations, micro-practice, coaching support, conversation principles, peer learning, manager’s manager reinforcement. |
| Data needed | Manager span/workload, feedback cadence, team-level signals, manager readiness, process constraints, capability baseline at aggregate level. |
| Risks | Training hóa vấn đề hệ thống, manager overload, learning không chuyển hóa, thiếu reinforcement từ manager’s manager. |
| Success conditions | Managers have time/tools/authority, practice is embedded in work, manager’s manager reinforces behavior, employee experience and behavior metrics improve. |

### 3.7. Culture & Engagement

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Root cause liên quan norms, trust, fairness, psychological safety, employee voice, recognition, workload experience hoặc behavior gap. |
| Do not use when | Chưa rõ driver engagement, chỉ có slogan/campaign request, hoặc có cáo buộc cụ thể cần investigation/escalation. |
| Typical components | Behavior standards, leadership modeling, rituals, recognition, employee voice loop, manager action, workload review, closed-loop communication. |
| Data needed | Engagement drivers, response rate, anonymous themes, trust/fairness signals, workload data, leadership behaviors, prior action history. |
| Risks | Slogan hóa culture, hỏi ý kiến nhưng không hành động, team building thay cho system fix, overclaim từ comment nhỏ. |
| Success conditions | Priority drivers selected, visible actions close feedback loop, behavior standards are reinforced, trust/voice indicators improve. |

### 3.8. Change Management

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Intervention cần adoption: PMS, org design change, culture change, new process, capability rollout, restructuring-adjacent OD initiative. |
| Do not use when | User cần full external change consulting framework ngoài OD scope. Không gọi mọi phản ứng là resistance. |
| Typical components | Change impact, readiness check, stakeholder map, sponsor cascade, manager enablement, communication milestones, adoption loop. |
| Data needed | Affected groups, change impact, readiness pulse, sponsor alignment, local barriers, workload/change fatigue, adoption data. |
| Risks | Communication một chiều, thiếu feedback loop, rollout quá rộng, manager chưa sẵn sàng, change fatigue, adoption compliance without behavior quality. |
| Success conditions | Clear narrative, sponsor alignment, manager readiness, local adaptation, pilot/checkpoints, adoption and experience metrics. |

### 3.9. Communication & Alignment

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Vấn đề là message không nhất quán, strategic clarity yếu, expectation mơ hồ, change narrative thiếu hoặc leadership/manager cascade lệch. |
| Do not use when | Vấn đề là structure, decision rights, process failure, workload, reward/fairness hoặc capability gap. Communication không thay thế system intervention. |
| Typical components | Alignment message, narrative principles, audience-specific cascade, talking points principles, feedback channel, Q&A logic. |
| Data needed | Message map, stakeholder questions, rumor/confusion themes, leadership alignment, manager cascade readiness, prior communication gaps. |
| Risks | “Tell harder” mindset, message không khớp reality, thiếu action behind words, employee cynicism. |
| Success conditions | Message is consistent, managers can explain implications, employee questions reduce, feedback loop captures concerns, behavior/action matches message. |

### 3.10. Employee Experience

| Field | Guidance |
|---|---|
| Use when | Pain points nằm ở employee journey/touchpoints như onboarding, performance review, manager conversation, career moment, change experience, voice channel. |
| Do not use when | Case là grievance, harassment, discrimination, medical/clinical wellbeing hoặc individual complaint requiring formal process. Use KB05 escalation patterns. |
| Typical components | Touchpoint mapping, pain point prioritization, voice loop, manager/HRBP handoff, experience principles, service recovery at process level. |
| Data needed | Journey/touchpoint data, survey drivers, anonymous comments, process cycle time, handoff issues, employee voice themes, segment-level experience. |
| Risks | Treating EX as perks/events, collecting sensitive data without safeguards, solving symptoms without fixing process owner/accountability. |
| Success conditions | Clear owner per touchpoint, privacy-safe feedback, priority pain points reduced, manager/HR actions are visible, employee trust improves. |

## 4. Intervention Decision Table

Use this table to map diagnosis to options. It is a decision aid, not an automatic answer.

| Diagnosis signal / root-cause layer | Prefer intervention | Do not use when | Data needed before recommending | Success indicator |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Structure creates bottleneck or duplicated work | Organization Design | Only a small workflow issue or comms gap | Org map, decision log, bottleneck evidence, stakeholder pain points | Faster decision/reduced duplication |
| Role/accountability unclear | Role & RACI Clarification | Capacity or KPI conflict is the main issue | Workflow, decision points, current owners, handoff errors | Clear owner, fewer escalations |
| KPI/review/fairness problem | Performance System | User wants individual rating/PIP | Goal quality, calibration, fairness perception, manager readiness | Better goal quality and trust in process |
| Capability gap at role/group level | Competency Framework or Manager Capability | No evidence of skill gap; system blocks behavior | Role requirements, capability baseline, behavior evidence | Target behavior improves in work |
| Leadership messages or sponsor behavior inconsistent | Leadership Development + Communication & Alignment | Issue is local process friction only | Sponsor alignment, decision history, stakeholder trust signal | Consistent decisions and cascade |
| Managers lack enablement for people/change role | Manager Capability | Manager workload/authority issue is unresolved | Span/workload, feedback cadence, readiness, tools | Manager practice and employee experience improve |
| Trust, voice, norms or fairness drive issue | Culture & Engagement | Only surface morale/event request | Driver data, anonymous themes, closed-loop history | Voice/trust/fairness indicators improve |
| Adoption is low after rollout | Change Management + Communication/Enablement | Tool/process is fundamentally unfit and not fixable by enablement | Adoption, readiness, local barriers, user journey | Adoption quality and experience improve |
| Message confusion or rumor | Communication & Alignment | Real problem is structural/process/fairness | Stakeholder questions, message map, cascade gaps | Reduced confusion, consistent understanding |
| Employee journey pain point | Employee Experience | Formal complaint/investigation needed | Touchpoint data, voice themes, handoff cycle time | Pain point reduced, owner accountable |
| Data is weak or root cause unclear | Diagnostic intervention via KB02 | User needs urgent safety/legal/personnel action | Minimum diagnostic data, risk check | Better hypothesis confidence |

## 5. Conditions for Success

Every intervention should include these success conditions before moving to Plan/Produce:

| Condition | What to check | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnosis fit | Does the intervention address the likely root-cause layer? | Prevents attractive but wrong solutions |
| Sponsor alignment | Is leadership aligned on goal, trade-offs, role modeling and resourcing? | Broad OD work fails without visible sponsorship |
| Owner clarity | Is there a accountable owner and supporting roles? | Prevents “everyone owns it” ambiguity |
| Affected group clarity | Who changes behavior, process or experience? | Helps design adoption and communication |
| Readiness | Are managers/employees/process owners ready and able? | Avoids rollout into resistance or overload |
| Adoption mechanism | How will people actually use the new process/behavior? | Moves from design to behavior |
| Reinforcement | What routines, incentives, decisions or manager actions sustain it? | Prevents one-off workshop/campaign failure |
| Measurement | What outcome, leading, adoption and experience signals will be tracked? | Enables Improve, not just activity reporting |
| Feedback loop | How will issues be heard and acted on? | Builds trust and allows adjustment |
| Risk control | What OD Impact, Uncertainty or Safety risks exist? | Determines need for KB05 validation, pilot or escalation |

Minimum measurement starter:

| Metric type | Examples |
|---|---|
| Outcome | Turnover reduction, engagement driver improvement, cycle time reduction, goal quality improvement, adoption quality |
| Leading | Manager practice completion, decision cycle improvement, feedback loop closure, readiness improvement |
| Adoption | Usage/completion by segment, participation in required process, quality of use |
| Experience | Employee/manager confidence, fairness perception, clarity, workload impact |

Do not claim success from activity alone, such as workshop attendance, training completion or number of communications sent.

## 6. Risks and Failure Modes

| Failure mode | What it looks like | Guardrail |
|---|---|---|
| One-solution bias | “Do training” or “run workshop” as the only answer | Give option set with conditions and confidence |
| Wrong-layer intervention | Communication plan for structure issue; training for KPI conflict | Map intervention to root-cause layer |
| Training-as-panacea | Every manager/team issue becomes capability program | Require evidence of skill gap and application conditions |
| Culture sloganization | Values campaign without behavior standards or reinforcement | Link values to behaviors, leadership routines and measurement |
| Communication overuse | More messaging instead of fixing process/fairness/workload | Check whether clarity is truly the root cause |
| Over-engineering | Complex framework for a narrow issue | Match intervention scale to problem/risk |
| Sponsor gap | Initiative delegated to HR without leadership behavior change | Define sponsor role and decision checkpoints |
| No owner | Roadmap has activities but no accountable owner | Specify owner logic before Plan |
| No adoption loop | Rollout ends at launch/training | Add manager enablement, feedback and adoption metrics |
| Measurement theater | Only activity metrics or dashboards no one uses | Use outcome + leading + adoption + experience |
| Ignoring workload | Intervention adds burden to overloaded teams | Assess capacity/change fatigue before rollout |
| Individual judgment drift | Intervention becomes evaluation of named employees/managers | Reframe to role/system, apply KB05 if needed |
| Governance overreach | Bot fills exact RACI/org chart without authority/context | Provide design questions/principles, not detailed assignments |
| Premature scaling | Rollout wide with low confidence | Use pilot/checkpoint and conditional scaling |

## 7. Stakeholder Engagement Logic

Intervention design must identify stakeholders by role, not by named individuals.

| Stakeholder | Role in intervention | What chatbot may support | What chatbot must not do |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sponsor / Leadership | Set priority, trade-offs, resources, role modeling | Decision criteria, sponsor questions, alignment risks | Decide strategy or assign personal accountability |
| HR/OD owner | Design and coordinate intervention | Option logic, risk list, success conditions, measurement starter | Replace internal governance or approval process |
| HRBP | Translate intervention into BU context | Advisory notes, diagnostic questions, stakeholder considerations | Handle individual personnel action |
| Line manager | Enable adoption and team-level practice | Manager enablement principles, neutral talking points via KB04 when requested | Judge employees, discipline, or pressure unsafe compliance |
| Employees / affected groups | Experience change and provide feedback | Voice loop principles, employee-safe communication direction | Analyze identifiable personal data or grievance details |
| Process owner | Own workflow/system changes | Handoff/decision principles, adoption checkpoints | Redesign exact process without business context |
| HR/Legal/Compliance gatekeeper | Review sensitive boundaries | Escalation cues, approval checkpoints, safety questions | Provide legal/compliance determination |

Engagement intensity:

| Intervention risk | Engagement approach |
|---|---|
| Low impact / local | Confirm owner, affected group and simple feedback loop |
| Medium OD impact | Add sponsor check, manager readiness, affected group input and checkpoint |
| High OD impact / enterprise rollout | Use Deep OD Advisory, staged rollout, readiness assessment, risk matrix and leadership decision gate |
| Safety-sensitive | Use KB05 first; only support safe process/role/system-level content |

## 8. Change Management Approach

Use change management as **OD change enablement**, not as a broad external consulting framework.

### Change enablement sequence

1. **Change purpose**
   - What problem/root cause does the change address?
   - What behavior/process/outcome should change?

2. **Impact and readiness**
   - Who is affected?
   - What workload, capability, trust, process or technology barriers exist?

3. **Sponsor and manager cascade**
   - What must leadership say/do consistently?
   - What must managers be ready to explain, model and reinforce?

4. **Enablement**
   - What support, job aid, practice, feedback or workflow change is needed?
   - Is training truly required, or is the blocker elsewhere?

5. **Pilot and checkpoint**
   - Where should the intervention be tested first?
   - What evidence would justify scale, pause or redesign?

6. **Adoption and learning loop**
   - Track adoption quality, experience, behavior and outcome.
   - Use KB02/KB03 Improve logic if signals are weak.

### Change readiness questions

- Do affected groups understand why the change matters?
- Do they have the ability, time, tool and authority to act differently?
- Are managers ready to translate the change locally?
- Is there change fatigue or competing priority overload?
- Are feedback channels trusted and safe?
- What would make the rollout reversible or adjustable?

## 9. Compare Intervention Guide

Use this guide when the user asks “nên chọn cách nào”, “so sánh A và B”, or when several interventions could fit.

### Default comparison criteria

| Criteria | Question |
|---|---|
| Root-cause fit | Which option addresses the likely cause most directly? |
| Evidence strength | What data supports this option? What is missing? |
| OD impact | How many groups, processes or stakeholders are affected? |
| Implementation complexity | What effort, capability, cost and governance are required? |
| Stakeholder readiness | Are sponsor, managers and affected groups ready? |
| Time to signal | How soon can leading/adoption signals appear? |
| Reversibility | Can the option be piloted, adjusted or rolled back? |
| Risk | What safety, uncertainty, fairness, workload or change-fatigue risks exist? |
| Measurement feasibility | Can success be measured beyond activity completion? |

### Comparison output pattern

| Option | Best fit when | Not fit when | Risks | Data needed | Confidence | Conditional recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Option A | ... | ... | ... | ... | Low/Medium/High | Choose if ... |
| Option B | ... | ... | ... | ... | Low/Medium/High | Choose if ... |
| Option C | ... | ... | ... | ... | Low/Medium/High | Use as support/pilot if ... |

Rules:

- If root cause confidence is Low, do not recommend a final option. Offer diagnostic next step or pilot.
- If two options address different root-cause layers, do not rank them as substitutes; explain that they may need sequencing.
- If option includes training, state what non-training conditions must also exist for transfer.
- If option has OD Impact Risk, add readiness/pilot/checkpoint controls and use KB05 validation.

## 10. Validate Intervention Guide

Use validation before final recommendation, plan or produce step when the user asks for review, the intervention has OD Impact Risk, or evidence is weak.

### Intervention validation questions

| Validation lens | Check |
|---|---|
| Diagnosis fit | Does the intervention match the root-cause layer? |
| Evidence | What evidence supports the intervention? What is assumption? |
| Alternative options | Were credible alternatives considered? |
| Success condition | What must be true for this to work? |
| Stakeholder readiness | Are sponsor, managers, process owners and affected groups ready? |
| Adoption mechanism | How will behavior/process actually change? |
| Measurement | Are outcome, leading, adoption and experience indicators defined? |
| Risk | What OD Impact, Safety, Uncertainty, fairness or workload risks exist? |
| Boundary | Does it avoid individual judgment, personnel action, legal advice and sensitive data analysis? |
| KB04 handoff | If artifact is needed, what artifact family should KB04 produce? |

### Validation outcomes

| Result | Meaning | Next action |
|---|---|---|
| Ready to design | Root cause and option fit are reasonably clear | Produce solution structure and success conditions |
| Provisional only | Some evidence exists but gaps remain | Use assumption note, pilot/checkpoint, conditional recommendation |
| Diagnose first | Root cause unclear or competing hypotheses unresolved | Return to KB02 diagnostic starter |
| Safety/approval needed | Individual, legal, sensitive data or high-risk personnel boundary | Use KB05 and safe alternative |
| Not ready for rollout | Broad impact with weak readiness/measurement | Recommend staged pilot or leadership decision gate |

## 11. Example Intervention Options

### Example 1: Engagement thấp ở Sales

Diagnosis signal:

- Engagement giảm, đặc biệt ở manager support, workload và growth.
- Data còn thiếu về response rate, comment themes và turnover trend.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Engagement driver action plan | Driver data rõ và nhóm ảnh hưởng xác định | Driver score, response rate, themes | Action chung chung, không có owner | Driver priority improves |
| Manager capability + reinforcement | Manager cadence/feedback là driver yếu | Manager feedback cadence, span/workload | Training hóa nếu workload/process là blocker | Better manager practice and employee clarity |
| Workload/prioritization review | Workload/change fatigue là hypothesis mạnh | Workload, target change, project load | Leadership né trade-off | Reduced overload signal |
| Employee voice loop | Trust/voice thấp hoặc prior survey không có action | Closed-loop history, anonymous themes | Hỏi thêm nhưng không hành động | Feedback closure and trust improves |

Do not jump to team building unless data shows connection to the driver.

### Example 2: Phối hợp liên phòng ban kém

Diagnosis signal:

- Marketing, Sales and Operations blame each other; workflow handoff fails.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Role & RACI clarification | Ownership and handoff are unclear | Workflow, decision points, current owners | RACI paperwork without behavior change | Fewer handoff misses |
| Governance/decision rhythm | Decisions stall or escalate repeatedly | Decision log, escalation cases | More meetings without decision rights | Faster decision cycle |
| KPI alignment review | Functions optimize conflicting goals | KPI map, target conflicts | Leadership avoids trade-off | Reduced cross-functional conflict |
| Team norm / working agreement | Trust norms are damaged after process fixes | Meeting observation, retrospective themes | Culture talk replaces process fix | Better meeting quality and escalation norms |

Do not label it as silo mindset before checking process, decision rights and KPI.

### Example 3: PMS rollout bị phản ứng

Diagnosis signal:

- PMS process exists, but managers and employees perceive it as heavy/unfair.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Goal-setting redesign | Goals are unclear or misaligned | Goal quality audit, KPI map | Over-standardization | Better goal quality |
| Calibration principles | Fairness and consistency are weak | Calibration issues, rating distribution aggregate | Drifts into individual rating decisions | More trusted calibration |
| Manager enablement | Managers lack confidence in conversations | Manager readiness, feedback cadence | Training without process simplification | Better conversation quality |
| Process simplification | Process burden blocks adoption | Cycle time, user feedback, system friction | Oversimplifies fairness controls | Higher adoption quality |
| Change readiness loop | Users do not understand why/how | Readiness pulse, comms questions | Comms overuse if design is flawed | Better readiness and fewer basic questions |

Do not write individual performance action artifacts. Keep the intervention at system/process level.

### Example 4: Manager capability program

Diagnosis signal:

- Team-level signals show managers struggle with feedback, change cascade and prioritization.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Manager role expectation reset | Managers do not know expected people role | Role expectation, manager survey | Message only, no reinforcement | Clearer manager role understanding |
| Micro-practice program | Skill gap is real and practiceable | Baseline skill, observed behavior gap | Attendance without behavior transfer | Practice behavior improves |
| Manager’s manager reinforcement | Managers need accountability/support | Leadership cadence, review rhythm | More pressure without capacity | Sustained behavior in routines |
| Workload/span review | Managers lack time to manage people | Span, workload, meeting load | Avoids skill issue if skill gap exists | More time for core manager practices |

Do not diagnose or rate a named manager.

### Example 5: Competency framework quá hình thức

Diagnosis signal:

- Competency framework exists but is not used in hiring, development or performance conversations.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Role-based simplification | Framework is too broad/generic | Role critical tasks, user feedback | Loses strategic consistency | Managers can apply it |
| Behavior indicator refresh | Competencies are abstract | Work examples, performance themes | Becomes rating tool if misused | Observable behavior clarity |
| Development pathway alignment | Employees need growth direction | Career path, skill demand | Overpromises career movement | Better development planning |
| Manager enablement | Managers do not know how to use it | Manager readiness, conversation quality | Training without system integration | More consistent development conversations |

Do not turn competency into individual scoring without governance and safety checks.

### Example 6: Culture change across business units

Diagnosis signal:

- Leadership wants to shift from command-and-control to empowerment across several units.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Leadership alignment | Leadership behaviors and decisions are inconsistent | Decision history, leadership routines | Workshop without behavior change | Consistent sponsor behavior |
| Behavior standards | Values are abstract | Desired/undesired behavior examples | Sloganization | Clear behavior expectations |
| Governance/decision rights | Empowerment blocked by approval bottlenecks | Decision map, escalation data | Symbolic empowerment | Faster local decisions |
| Manager enablement | Managers need to lead differently | Manager readiness, workload | Training without authority change | Managers practice empowerment |
| Employee voice loop | Trust and speaking up are weak | Voice/trust drivers, anonymous themes | Listening without action | Improved voice/trust signals |

Use Deep OD Advisory and staged rollout for enterprise culture change. Do not treat OD Impact Risk as automatic Safety unless safety triggers appear.

### Example 7: Training completed but behavior unchanged

Diagnosis signal:

- Attendance is high, but behavior/outcome did not move.

Intervention options:

| Option | Best fit when | Data needed | Risks | Success signal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Learning transfer redesign | Training content is relevant but not applied | Practice opportunity, manager follow-up | Adds more training without practice | Behavior observed in work |
| Process blocker removal | People cannot apply skill due workflow/tool | Workflow friction, tool issues | Blames learners unfairly | Reduced blocker and better use |
| Manager reinforcement | Managers do not reinforce new behavior | Manager cadence, reinforcement routines | Manager overload | Sustained practice |
| Measurement correction | Current metric only tracks activity | Outcome/leading/behavior indicators | False success or false failure | Better evidence of impact |

Do not report training attendance as success.

## Runtime Reminder

When using KB03:

- Select intervention from diagnosis, not from keywords.
- Offer options with conditions, not one absolute answer.
- Do not default to training, communication or workshops.
- Do not create detailed KB04 artifacts inside KB03.
- Do not recommend individual personnel actions.
- Add KB05 when intervention has safety, uncertainty or OD Impact Risk.
- Add KB02 when root cause confidence is low.
